About

I studied electrical engineering and neuroscience, and found my research home where the two meet: building interactive systems in which a robot's behavior is shaped by what a human intends, attends to, and feels. My master's work focused on EEG-based intent decoding for assistive robotic arms; along the way I became equally invested in the human side — how people build trust in a machine that acts on their neural signals.

Beyond the lab, I mentor undergraduate robotics teams and contribute to open-source EEG tooling. I believe good interaction research is equal parts signal processing, careful experiment design, and honest human evaluation.

01

EEG-Driven Shared Autonomy for Assistive Robotic Arms

BCI Shared Autonomy Motor Imagery

Motor-imagery EEG gives users a hands-free channel to steer a robotic arm, but raw decoding is too noisy for precise manipulation. This project blends decoded intent with goal inference and robot autonomy, arbitrating control continuously by decoder confidence. In a 12-participant study, shared control reduced task time by 34% and workload ratings significantly compared to direct decoding.

02

Error-Related Potentials as Implicit Feedback in Human–Robot Collaboration

BRI ErrP Online Learning

When a robot makes a mistake, the human brain reacts within a few hundred milliseconds — before any button press. We detect error-related potentials from single-trial EEG and use them as a free, implicit reward signal to correct robot actions online. The robot's policy adapts within a session, cutting repeated errors without any explicit user feedback.

03

Multimodal Intent Estimation for Socially-Aware Robot Navigation

HRI Gaze Sensor Fusion

People signal where they are going long before they get there — with gaze, posture, and gait. This project fuses eye-tracking, body kinematics, and lightweight EEG features to predict pedestrian intent 1–2 seconds ahead, letting a mobile robot yield, pass, or follow in a way people read as polite rather than erratic.

04

Passive BCI for Workload-Adaptive Robot Assistance

BCI Cognitive Workload Adaptive Autonomy

A good collaborator helps more when you are overwhelmed and steps back when you are in flow. Using passive EEG markers of cognitive workload, this system continuously tunes how much a robot teammate takes over during a joint assembly task — raising assistance under high load and yielding control under low load, without the user ever asking.
